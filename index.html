Pr. Neil Turok

"I see this gap which is that a young African with let's say first class degree in Mathematics in Africa would have no way to continue and to me this looks like tragedy. You've trained all this person all this way up and now they can't do anything and so I though let's do something to provide an avenue for them to progress. "

Neil Turock, interviewed by ARTE

  • 1958 Born the 16th Nov in Johannesburg - South Africa. His parents were activists in the anti-apartheid movement and the African National Congress.
  • 1983 Submitted his thesis "Strings and solitons in gauge theories" and gained his doctorate from Imperial College
  • 1992 He was awarded the Maxwell medal of the Institute of Physics for his contributions to theoretical physics
  • 1994 He was appointed Professor of Physics at Princeton University, then held the Chair of Mathematical Physics at the University of Cambridge starting in 1997.
  • 2003 Turok founded the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences in Cape Town. AIMS has since expanded to a network of six centres – in South Africa, Senegal, Ghana, Cameroon, Tanzania, and Rwanda – and has become Africa's most renowned institution for postgraduate training in mathematical science.
  • 2008 He was appointed Director of the Perimeter Institute.
  • 2013 He was re-appointed for a second term and additionally awarded the Mike and Ophelia Lazaridis Niels Bohr Chair at the institute.
  • 2018 Turok was named an Officer of the Order of Canada (Honorary) in recognition of his “substantial contributions as a scientist to the field of theoretical physics and cosmology.
